As you catch your breath after the fight, a faint, terrified cry echoes on the wind, 
carried from a cluster of crumbling ruins to the east. Peering through the heat shimmer, 
you see them: two small children, huddled in the shadow of a collapsed wall. 
Nearby, a massive, forked tongue flicks out from a dark doorway.
 A mutated Bandit, easily twenty feet long, is basking in the meager warmth, 
 its yellow eyes lazily watching the kids, playing with its food. 
 It's a side quest, a moment of moral choice in a world stripped of morality.
  You could ignore them, conserve your strength, and press on toward your primary goal. 
  But the memory of their terrified faces stays with you. Do you go to help them, 
  risking injury and revealing your position,
   or do you stay here and harden your heart to their plight?
